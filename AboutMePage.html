About Me

Howdy! my name is Fernando De La Fuente, and I started doing anything related to computer science in middle school where I made webpages and animations. I took a computer science class in my first year in high school which if I remember correctly, we used java. I went back to coding in my junior year with video game design. I made a simple platformer game, but I got to do everything from the animations of the character to the level design.

I graduated from Texas A&M University where I studied in Computer Science. I chose to minor in art since I wanted to learn what goes into frontend development. Despite this, I find more joy in backend development, but will not mind working in fullstack.

Outside of Computer Science, I like to play trombone. I spent all four years of high school playing in both the concert band and marching band. I even had the opportunity to perform in both Carnegie Hall and in the Macy's Thanksgiving Parade in New York in my sophomore year. I am glad I continue to play at A&M in the concert band, and admittedly, I am also glad I opted out of marching in the Aggie Band as I wanted to keep my hair.
